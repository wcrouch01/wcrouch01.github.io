A product of Silicon Valley, I always knew I would explore the world of technology. My initial, childhood love for the gaming world sparked my interest in technology, and I have followed that interest ever since. I learned to love not only the programs that I can create, but the process of creating them.

I started studying computer science my sophomore year at Menlo High School, and I took advanced courses during my final two years. My desire to go to one of the top computer science programs led me to University of Wisconsin-Madison. Living in the Midwest has been a life changing experience and great exposure to other parts of the country.

Through my classwork, I have gained experience in Java and C. Through independent study and work, I have grown in JavaScript, HTML and CSS, C#, and Python. As I enter my junior year at university, I have taken all of the core computer science courses and am eager to take advanced courses. I have taken courses in Data Structures, Machine Programming, Operating Systems, Statistics, Calculus, and Discrete Mathematics. I will be taking coursework in Artificial Intelligence, Algorithms and Optimization this year.

My goal is to become a software developer and make technology that impacts the world. I spent my first two college summers teaching programming to children at MV Code Club on the Bay Area peninsula. Through teaching, I have developed a greater understanding of my own work, as well as learned valuable business skills as a manager.

I also try to live a full life outside of the technology world. Throughout high school, I had a passion for water polo, and anchored one of the top teams in the country as goalie. I played all four years of high school and continued this passion playing on the club team at Wisconsin. I also am a big sports fan, and I am an avid fan of the Badgers and Golden State Warriors. I would love nothing more than to integrate these interests into my love of technology. I also joined Sigma Pi Fraternity and served as Housing Chair.

In my free time I enjoy working on programming projects, such as this website, keeping up with the news going on in the world, gaming, socializing, and I live my life to the fullest. Feel free to contact me, read about my work, and browse the features on this website.

    • CS537 (introduction to Operating Systems) programs Programs in CS537

    • xcheck.c: This program is a working file system checker. A checker reads in a file system image and makes sure that it is consistent.
    • pzip.c: This is a multithreaded run-length encoding compression program. Takes a file and compresses it using multiple worker threads.
    • xv6 lottery scheduler:T his program takes the standard xv6 program and changes the scheduler so that it uses a lottery scheduler with tickets. There is a graph inside demonstrating the results.
    • xv6 Multithreaded clone: This program takes the standard xv6 program and adds multithreaded features. Xv6 has a fork() function that splits code. This version has a clone() function that does a similar process except instead the clones share an address space with the parent. Includes many other differentiating factors from the fork() function.
    CS534 (Machine Organization and Programming) programs
    • mem.c: This program is a memory allocator in c. It initializes a memory space and from there can allocate and free memory blocks. The blocks come with block headers and adjacent free blocks are automatically coalesced.
    • csim.c: A cache simulator that can replay traces from Valgrind and output statistics such as number of hits, misses, and evictions. The replacement policy is LRU.
